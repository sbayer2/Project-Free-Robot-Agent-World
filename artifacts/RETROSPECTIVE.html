pseudo-marble retrospective — the F21 terminus

2026-07-21 · pseudo-marble v0.0.1 · F1–F21 · fidelity arc closed

The project now has a clean, honest headline: a shared latent learns a small, real coupling between appearance and behavior, but the prediction gain behind that coupling is shape-driven, not material-essence-driven. No render-fidelity lever rescues it.

What the project tested

pseudo-marble is a small, runnable instrument for the claim behind World Labs' Marble and Fei-Fei Li's taxonomy of world models: that a machine can hold one physical essence of an object from which both appearance and behavior are projected, rather than two separate decoders glued together.

The apparatus:

  • a controlled synthetic world (MuJoCo + continuous materials),
  • a ~1M-parameter shared-latent model (images → z → render/behavior/essence heads),
  • a coherence metric: when you nudge z, do look and behavior move together?
  • honest controls: untrained shared baseline, independent disjoint-latent baseline, held-out essence regions.

What we found

The only unconditional positive

F13: on pm_big, 20 seeds, lr 5e-4, the trained shared-latent model achieves learned coherence +0.146 (behavior) and +0.123 (essence), t ≈ 8.3 and 5.5. The disjoint-latent baseline sits near 0; the untrained shared baseline is ~0.10. Training genuinely produces a small shared structure.

The correction that reframes it

F18: the same model's held-out behavior gain over predict-mean is 1.413×. A shape-only oracle — which never sees density, friction, or restitution — scores 1.331×, and its 95% CI [1.01, 1.79] contains every trained seed. The fair ceiling, shape + actual visual appearance parameters, is 2.311×.

So the model extracts roughly 8% of the reachable essence signal. The coupling in F13 is real, but largely shape-driven, not material-essence-driven.

The reality test

F14/F16 ran the instrument on real ABO products. Result: VOID by preregistered gates. With friction/restitution assumed constant, the probes are nearly mass-blind, held-out prediction fails, and coherence is uninterpretable. The real-object question remains unanswered.

What this means

The headline is now a calibrated negative:

On a synthetic world with a deliberately subtle appearance↔physics coupling, a shared latent learns a small, real, shape-driven coupling. It does not learn the hidden material essence. Improving render fidelity, removing appearance noise, or adding an appearance auxiliary does not change this.

This is a useful result: it defines where the shared-latent idea stops working in a controlled setting and prevents overclaiming on similarly subtle couplings.

Strategic fork

  1. Accept the negative and stop. F18–F21 is a complete, preregistered, multi-seed finding. Ship it.
  2. Build a stronger-coupled synthetic world. To study essence learning, materials.py must make the coupling legible by design: stronger roughness↔friction, metallic↔density, informative visual cues, and a behavior target that isolates essence from shape. This is a new research phase, not a tweak.

Honest limits

  • The coupling is authored, not measured from reality.
  • The real-object test is VOID, not negative — no measured contact parameters exist in public datasets.
  • The model is small by design (~1M params). This result does not rule out larger models on richer worlds; it only says this controlled world does not admit essence learning.
